Things I Do

My passion lies in exploring, researching, and applying techniques for preserving and recovering heritage. With years of scientific experience in implementing these methods, I am deeply committed to uncovering and investigating the following key areas:

  • Conservation: This involves the careful maintenance and repair of cultural artifacts, buildings, monuments, and sites to prevent deterioration and damage.
  • Restoration: Restoration aims to return a heritage asset to its original condition or a specific historical period, often involving extensive research and skilled craftsmanship.
  • Documentation: Comprehensive documentation through photographs, drawings, and written records helps in understanding, monitoring, and preserving heritage assets.
  • Preventive conservation: This involves proactive measures such as environmental control, pest management, and handling protocols to prevent damage to heritage objects and sites.
  • Digital preservation: Utilizing digital technologies to create virtual replicas or archives of heritage assets, ensuring their accessibility and preservation in the digital age.
  • Community involvement: Engaging local communities in heritage preservation efforts fosters a sense of ownership and stewardship, leading to sustainable conservation practices.
  • Adaptive reuse: Repurposing heritage buildings and sites for contemporary uses while respecting their historic significance helps ensure their continued relevance and sustainability.
  • Education and outreach: Promoting awareness and appreciation of heritage through educational programs, exhibitions, and outreach activities encourages public support for preservation efforts.

A Few Projects

During my undergraduate and graduate studies and as a conservator with the National Heritage Office, I've been involved in numerous projects dedicated to researching, developing, and applying diverse heritage preservation methods. "Preserving the Archaeological Legacy: Conservation of King Sahle Selassie of Shoa's Palace Ruins in Angolala"

In 2019, I worked as a conservator and project coordinator on the conservation of the archaeological palace ruins of King Sahle Selassie of Shoa in Angolala. King Sahle Selassie, the grandfather of Menelik II, built the palace in 1844. UNESCO recognizes his letter to Queen Elizabeth of England as one of Ethiopia’s literary heritage; he had an interest in Western technology and established trade and friendship agreements with France and Great Britain. The project aimed to strengthen the fragile structural elements of the ruins. Despite being a challenging 3-month endeavor, it offered a chance to preserve this forgotten historical heritage site for future generations.

Immersive Telerobotics in hazardous or inaccessible enviroments

Firefighting is one domain where immersive telerobotics can play a crucial role in enhancing the safety and effectiveness of firefighting operations. Our lab did this project with the Italian institutional agency for fire and rescue service (Vigili del Fuoco).

MR Interface for Immersive Remote Telerobotics

This immersive remote telerobotic interface presents a significant leap forward in the field of human-robot interaction. We have created a robust system that enables operators to control robots remotely while experiencing immersion and presence.

Foveated Rendering for Immersive Remote Visualization

Network latency and throughput requirements negatively impact user's experience. The human visual system was taken as an inspiration to design a novel immersive remote visualization system to improve transmission, interaction and visualization in VR.
